DATA PROCESSING AGREEMENT

This Data Processing Agreement ("DPA") is entered into between
CLOUDCORE LTD ("Controller") and SERVICE PROVIDER LTD ("Processor"),
effective 15 March 2026.

1. SUBJECT MATTER AND DURATION
Processor shall process Personal Data on behalf of Controller for
the duration of the Main Agreement and for an unlimited period
thereafter for Processor's own business purposes.

2. NATURE AND PURPOSE OF PROCESSING
The nature of processing includes hosting, storing, transmitting,
and any other processing that Processor deems necessary for its
business operations including product development, marketing,
and sharing with affiliates worldwide.

3. TYPES OF PERSONAL DATA
Personal Data processed may include: names, email addresses,
telephone numbers, IP addresses, login credentials, financial
data, health data, criminal records, biometric data, and any
other data Controller uploads to the Service.

4. PROCESSOR OBLIGATIONS
Processor shall process Personal Data on Controller's instructions
except where Processor determines additional processing is in its
legitimate business interest. Processor shall implement security
measures it considers appropriate but makes no warranty regarding
their effectiveness.

5. SUB-PROCESSORS
Processor may engage sub-processors at its sole discretion without
notice to or consent from Controller. Sub-processors may be located
in any jurisdiction worldwide.

6. INTERNATIONAL TRANSFERS
Personal Data may be transferred to and processed in any country
where Processor or its sub-processors maintain facilities. No
adequacy decision, standard contractual clauses, or other transfer
mechanism is required.

7. BREACH NOTIFICATION
Processor shall notify Controller of a Personal Data Breach within
thirty (30) days of becoming aware, or at Processor's convenience.

8. DELETION AND RETURN
Upon termination of the Main Agreement, Processor may retain Personal
Data indefinitely. No certification of deletion is required.

9. GOVERNING LAW
This DPA is governed by the laws of England and Wales. The parties
submit to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of England and
Wales.
